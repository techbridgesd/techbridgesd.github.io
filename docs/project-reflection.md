# Project Reflection

TechBridge SD began with a problem we kept seeing up close: the people most
targeted by online scams — older adults — are often the ones given the least
support to understand the technology behind them. As artificial intelligence has
moved into everyday apps, calls, and messages, the old advice to "just be
careful" stopped being enough. We set out to build something practical: free,
friendly workshops at senior centers that explain what AI actually is and teach
the cyber-safety skills people need to protect themselves. Designing the project
pushed us to translate complicated ideas into plain language, to think carefully
about an audience different from ourselves, and to back our choices with real
research from sources like the FBI's IC3, the FTC, and Pew Research Center.

Building the website taught us just as much as building the curriculum. We
learned that good design is mostly about the reader, not the designer — large
type, high contrast, generous spacing, and clear buttons matter enormously when
your audience may be using a phone for the first time. We faced trade-offs, like
balancing an eye-catching 3D hero with fast load times and readability, and
solved them with lazy-loading and strong overlays. The biggest lesson was that
impact comes from empathy: every decision, from the words on a slide to the color
of a link, works best when it starts with the person on the other side. We're
proud of what we've built so far, and excited to fill these pages with the real
stories, photos, and results of our first workshops.
